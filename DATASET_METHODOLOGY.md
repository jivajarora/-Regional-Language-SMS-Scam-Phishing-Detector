# Dataset Methodology Report

This document outlines the design, sourcing, and data-augmentation strategy for the dataset used to train the Regional-Language SMS Phishing Detector.

---

## 1. Sourcing Strategy

The dataset is built from a core of manually curated "seed" messages representing standard transactional SMS templates and real-world mobile scam hooks prevalent in India.

### A. Scam Message Seeds (`real_scam`)
Scam patterns were sourced from public reports of financial fraud in India (cybersecurity warnings, banking alerts, Twitter reports, and consumer protection forums). These cover four high-frequency mobile fraud archetypes:
1. **KYC Disconnection Fraud**: Fake bank or e-wallet alerts claiming that the user's account is blocked/suspended and requiring immediate KYC updates via a malicious link.
2. **Lottery/Prize Scams**: Alerts claiming the user has won a cash lottery or cashback prize, requiring them to call a mobile number.
3. **Overdue Payments/Refunds**: Threat alerts claiming credit card bills are overdue and threatening card blockage within 24 hours, or fake tax/UPI refund offers.
4. **Part-time Job Scams**: Job recruitment offers promising high daily earnings for minimal work, directing targets to WhatsApp numbers.

### B. Legitimate Message Seeds (`legitimate`)
Legitimate message templates were sourced from personal SMS logs containing actual notifications sent by banks, payment platforms, and delivery systems:
1. **One-Time Passwords (OTPs)**: Standard banking/login verification templates.
2. **Transaction Credit/Debit Alerts**: Real-world alerts showing money credited or debited, complete with reference and transaction IDs.
3. **Delivery Notifications**: Notifications from delivery services containing tracking links and verification PINs.
4. **Utility Bill Generations**: Legitimate monthly bills generated by telecom or utility providers.

## 2. Data Augmentation (Synthetic Generation)

Indian SMS scams are highly localized and heavily code-mixed (Hindi written in Roman script - Hinglish), meaning standard NLP spam corpora (which are almost exclusively English-only) are ineffective. Due to privacy concerns and the lack of publicly available Indian SMS corpora, we employ **defensive data augmentation** in [build_dataset.py](file:///d:/Summer%20Projects/Regional-Language%20PhishingScam%20Detector/build_dataset.py) to expand the seed dataset to a size suitable for baseline model training.

### Augmentation Process
For each seed template in [seed_messages.json](file:///d:/Summer%20Projects/Regional-Language%20PhishingScam%20Detector/seed_messages.json), we programmatically generated **12 unique variants** using:
1. **Placeholder Substitution**: Templates use curly-bracket placeholders (like `{bank}`, `{amount}`, `{phone}`, `{link}`, `{otp}`) which are randomly swapped with items from structured entity pools (e.g. rotating banks like SBI, HDFC, ICICI, or UPI apps like Paytm and PhonePe).
2. **Synonym/Variation Swapping**: Using a predefined script-specific dictionary, terms are randomly replaced with semantic synonyms (e.g. in Hinglish: `suspend` $\leftrightarrow$ `block`, `turant` $\leftrightarrow$ `jaldi`; in Hindi: `तुरंत` $\leftrightarrow$ `जल्दी`, `ब्लॉक` $\leftrightarrow$ `बंद`).
3. **Random Sender Perturbation**: Sender headers are generated matching standard shortcodes (e.g. `56767`), alpha-headers (e.g. `VK-HDFCBK`), or regular mobile numbers to train the model to distinguish sender profiles.

## 3. Dataset Distribution

Following data augmentation, the final [dataset.csv](file:///d:/Summer%20Projects/Regional-Language%20PhishingScam%20Detector/dataset.csv) contains **312 records** with the following class balance and script distributions:

* **Class Balance**: 
  - `scam`: 156 records (50.0%)
  - `legit`: 156 records (50.0%)
* **Language Distribution**:
  - `english`: 104 records (33.3%)
  - `hindi`: 104 records (33.3%)
  - `hinglish`: 104 records (33.3%)
* **Source Categories**:
  - `real_scam` (Original Scam Seeds): 12 records
  - `synthetic_scam` (Augmented Scams): 144 records
  - `legitimate` (Original & Augmented Legit Messages): 156 records

## 4. Known Limitations

While this dataset provides a solid foundation for baseline training, reviewers and interviewers should note the following limitations:

1. **Semantic Drift & New Scam Hooks**: Mobile scams evolve rapidly. This dataset is restricted to classic hooks (KYC, jobs, lottery, refunds). It does not capture newer scam formats (such as fake electricity bill disconnections, traffic challan notices, or package customs fraud) unless new seeds are manually added.
2. **Small Scale**: With only 312 records, the vocabulary is small. Downstream classifiers are highly sensitive to specific terms (like "KYC" or "lottery") and may misclassify messages that lack these exact words but carry the same scam intent.
3. **Hinglish Spelling Coverage**: Hinglish is written phonetically with massive variation (e.g., *turant*, *trnt*, *turent*). While our spelling standardizer captures major variations, it cannot be exhaustive.
4. **Lack of Dialect Diversity**: Hindi is written and spoken with regional dialects (Bihari, Haryanvi, etc.) that introduce different slang and structures. The dataset currently reflects standard urban Hindi/Hinglish templates only.
5. **Legitimate Warnings (False Positives)**: Security alerts sent by banks warning users *not* to share OTPs will likely trigger false positive scam classifications, because they contain multiple scam keywords. Distinguishing a threat alert from a threat warning requires deeper semantic parsing.
